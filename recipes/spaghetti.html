Spaghetti with Marinara Sauce Recipe

How To Make Spaghetti

This classic Italian-American dish features al dente spaghetti tossed in a rich, flavorful marinara sauce. It's a comforting and satisfying meal perfect for any night of the week.

What is Marinara sauce?

Marinara sauce is a simple, quick-cooking tomato sauce typically made with tomatoes, garlic, herbs (like basil, oregano, and parsley), and onions. It's a staple in Italian-American cuisine and serves as a versatile base for many dishes, including pasta, pizza, and subs. The term "marinara" means "mariner's style" in Italian, suggesting its origin as a sauce for sailors.

Spaghetti with Marinara Sauce Ingredients

These are the simple, fresh ingredients you’ll need to make this homemade spaghetti with marinara sauce:

  • Basil
  • Cheese
  • Oil
  • Nuts
  • Garlic
  • Lemon juice
  • Spaghetti
  • Canned crushed tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Dried oregano
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps to follow

  1. Prepare the Aromatics

    • Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onion and sauté until softened and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
    • Add minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.

  2. Simmer the Sauce

    • Pour in the canned crushed tomatoes.
    • Stir in dried oregano, salt and black pepper to taste.
    • Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce heat to low, cover, and let it cook for at least 30 minutes (or longer for more flavor development), stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
    • Add fresh basil leaves during the last 5-10 minutes of simmering for a fresh flavor.

  3. Cook the Spaghetti

    • While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil.
    • Add the spaghetti nd cook according to package directions until al dente (firm to the bite).
    • Before draining, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water. This starchy water can be used to thin the sauce if needed and help it cling to the pasta.
    • Drain the spaghetti thoroughly.

  4. Combine and Serve

    • Add the drained spaghetti directly to the pot with the marinara sauce.
    • Toss well to coat the spaghetti evenly with the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water until it reaches your desired consistency.
    • Serve hot, garnished with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and extra fresh basil, if desired.

Enjoy your classic homemade Spaghetti with Marinara Sauce!
